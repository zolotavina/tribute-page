Coco Chanel

Leading French modernist designer, whose patterns of simplicity and style revolutionised women's clothing

History of Coco Chanel:

  • 1883 - Before she became known as “Coco,” she was just Gabrielle born in Saumur, France, a market town looking over the Loire river.
  • 1895 - Coco's mother died, and her father abandoned all five children
  • 1901 - Chanel leaves the orphanage to live in a catholic finishing school
  • 1903 - Coco gets a job working as a seamstress
  • 1907 - Gabrielle earns her nickname “Coco”
  • 1908 - Chanel is a kept woman and starts selling hats from fer lover's apartment
  • 1910 - Opens her first shop: a hat shop, “Mode Chanel”
  • 1913 - Opens her second store in Deauville, “Gabrielle Chanel”
  • 1915 - Opens her first haute couture boutique in Biarritz, calls it "Chanel"
  • 1916 - Chanel is featured in Harpers Bazaar
  • 1919 - Opens fourth boutique in Paris
  • 1921 - Coco launches CHANEL N°5
  • 1922 - Coco launches CHANEL N°22
  • 1924 - Coco launches her first makeup collection
  • 1925 - Creates the iconic logo of interlocking C's
  • 1925 - Chanel launched the tweed Chanel: something no one had ever seen women wear
  • 1926 - Chanel introduces the LBD, Little Black Dress
  • 1939 - Due to the war, Chanel closes her shops
  • 1945 - 1953 - Chanel self exiles to Lausanne, Switzerland, to escape prosecution and persecution
  • 1954 - Returns to Paris after 15 years but loses ownership of her company
  • 1955 - Chanel changes handbag history and ads a shoulder strap to her quilted flap purse
  • 1971 - At 87 years old, Coco Chanel dies in her bed at the Ritz Hotel in Paris, France

"Fashion fades, only style remains."

-- Coco Chanel
